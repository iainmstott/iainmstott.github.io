IAIN STOTT

I'm a computational evolutionary ecologist, interested in feedbacks between the structures of ecological systems, their dynamics, and their function. My work is designed to facilitate better forecasts of how ecological systems change over time, how better to manage them, and answer fundamental questions about how they have evolved to work.

My current project is called "WHYAGE", funded by Marie Sk≈Çodowska-Curie Actions and based at the University of Southern Denmark. I also currently sit on the Journal of Ecology's associate editorial board, the British Ecological Society's Publications Committee, and their Equality and Diversity Working Group.

The Labord's chameleon grows up to 30cm long, but lives for only 4-5 months: the shortest lifespan for a tetrapod vertabrate. It is a semelparous organism, meaning it only breeds once (in late summer) after which it dies. The eggs incubate over winter and hatch the following spring. Photo by Frank Vassen.
